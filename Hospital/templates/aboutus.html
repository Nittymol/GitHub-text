Mary Queens Mission Hospital

To strive for meaningful existence by providing selfless and quality service and uncompromising commitment to all irrespective of caste and creed.

Mary Queens Mission Hospital founded in 1957, as a charitable institution, is the living expression of the apostolic concern and social responsibility of Carmelites of Mary Immaculate (CMI), the first indigenous congregation in India in the Catholic Church. The hospital, as the premier project of Mary Queens Mission Hospital Trust, a registered charitable organization, is owned and managed by the CMI St. Joseph Province, Kottayam.
